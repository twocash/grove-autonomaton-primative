# Eagle Creek Golf Club

## Profile
- **Type:** Venue
- **Role:** Home course, primary practice facility
- **Location:** Indianapolis, IN
- **Par:** 72
- **Yardage:** 6,400 (white tees — what the boys play)

## Course Character
Eagle Creek is a public course but plays like a private club on quiet weekday afternoons. Tree-lined fairways, well-maintained greens, and enough water to demand respect. It's where our guys learn to play real golf — not range golf.

## Key Holes for Coaching
- **Hole 4 (Par 4, 380 yds):** Prevailing west wind makes this play longer than it looks. Tests driving accuracy. Good early-match barometer.
- **Hole 7 (Par 3, 165 yds):** Water right, bunker left. Pure target golf. This is where Danny's nerves show up.
- **Hole 12 (Par 5, 510 yds):** Reachable in two for Jake and Tommy. Decision hole — do you go for it or lay up? Course management test.
- **Hole 14 (Par 4, 405 yds):** Exposed to wind, narrow landing area. The grind hole. Character shows here.
- **Hole 18 (Par 4, 390 yds):** Slight dogleg right with water guarding the green. Match-defining hole. Practice this one weekly.

## Practice Areas
- Driving range: 30 stalls, grass tees available spring-fall
- Short game area: Chipping green + practice bunker
- Putting green: Two greens, different speeds. Use the back green for match prep — it's faster.

## Weather Patterns
- West wind dominates March-April, affects holes 4, 7, 14
- Greens run faster after dry spells — adjust putting drills accordingly
- Morning dew makes approach shots grab more — factor into early tee times

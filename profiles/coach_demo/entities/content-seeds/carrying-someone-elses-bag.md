# Carrying Someone Else's Bag

Pillar: caddie_servant
Theme: service

At our first team practice this spring, I paired each upperclassman with a freshman and told them: "Today you're his caddie. Carry his bag. Read his putts. Tell him what club to hit. Your score doesn't matter — his does." The juniors hated it for about three holes. Then something shifted. Tommy started reading greens for Will like it was the Masters. Jake was coaching Marcus through his pre-shot routine between shots. By the back nine, the upperclassmen were more invested in the freshmen's birdies than they'd ever been in their own. That's the lesson the caddie teaches: when you carry someone else's burden, yours gets lighter. Every time.
